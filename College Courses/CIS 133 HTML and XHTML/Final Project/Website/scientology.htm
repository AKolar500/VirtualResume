According to L. Ron Hubbard,

Approximately 75 million years ago, an alien named Xenu ruled an enormous Galactic Confederacy, consisting of several planets and stars. One day, Xenu's rule was threatened, so he devised a plan to deal with this threat. The goal was simple: reduce the population of his dominion down to a more manageable size so he could rule over what's left more effectively. The plan, however, was not as simple.

With the help of psychiatrists, Xenu gathered billions of his citizens under the pretense of income tax inspections, but as Admiral Ackbar put it: "It's a trap!" Xenu had every one of them paralyzed, then he had them frozen in a mixture of alcohol and glycol to ensure their souls would not leave their bodies as long as they were alive. Then, each of these aliens were transported to the planet of Teegeeack in Douglas DC-8 space planes, where they were to be summarily executed. How? Every one of the still-frozen aliens were placed at the bases of volcanoes. Next, Hydrogen bombs were lowered into each volcano and detonated simultaneously. Fortunately a few aliens survived these explosions, so they could not experience what happened next. However, for the ones who weren't so lucky, it was hell on earth.

The disembodied alien souls (hereafter referred to as thetans) had been blown into the sky by the blasts. Xenu's forces used an electronic ribbon to gather all of the thetans into several gigantic movie theaters, where they were forced to watch a 3D brainwashing movie for 36 days. During this time, a device called the R6 implant was inserted into the memories of all captured thetans. This device was programmed to do two things: 1) kill anyone who attempted to remember their former lives or the events that had just transpired, and 2) give the implanted thetan false religious memories (i.e. memories associated with God, Satan, and other imaginary deities).

After this process was over, these poor thetans began to cluster together into groups of a few thousand, having lost the ability to differentiate between each other as a result of the massive brainwashing process that just occurred. Each cluster of thetans entered the few bodies that survived the explosions. These possessed creatures became known as body thetans, and they continue to roam Teegeeack to this day, causing spiritual and physical harm to everything and everyone around them wherever they go.

Back to Xenu. Unfortunately, while this plan postponed Xenu's loss of power, it did not prevent it. Xenu and his allies were ousted from the Confederacy and imprisoned in some kind of electronic mountain trap, from which none have escaped. Teegeeack was abandoned by the Galactic Confederacy and continues to be a prison planet.

Now, what does Teegeeack look like now, and how can we avoid being killed on it by these body thetan abominations? Well, it doesn't look much different from our planet, which is surprising since prison planets would usually have no appealing structures or people on them, just the usual mountains of skulls and forests of impaled snitches. Furthermore, while it is possible to avoid being killed by some body thetans, it is impossible to avoid being killed by all body thetans. These monsters take all forms, as well. Some look like hillbillies, and others present themselves in such a way that they are guaranteed to get a few votes into office. Why is earth so similar to Teegeeack?
